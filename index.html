Steps to reduce anxiety

Quick Guide

During the COVID-19 pandemic, fear and anxiety about a new illness and what might happen can be overwhelming in both adults and children. Public health measures, such as social distancing, can make people feel isolated and lonely and may increase stress and anxiety. However, these measures are necessary to reduce the spread of COVID-19. Dealing with stress in a healthy way will strengthen you and your loved ones.

Healthy alternatives to cope with stress:

Be informed:

Know what to do if you feel sick and are concerned about COVID-19. Contact a healthcare professional before self-medicating for COVID-19. Know where and how to access treatment and other resources and support services, such as counseling or therapy (in person or through telehealth services).

Take care of your emotional health:

Taking care of your emotional health will help you think clearly and react to the urgent need to protect yourself and your family. Take breaks and stop watching, reading, or listening to the news. This includes social media. Hearing about the pandemic repeatedly can affect you. Connect with your faith and community organizations. While social distancing measures have been put in place, consider connecting online, through social media, by phone, or by mail.

Take care of your body.

Take deep breaths, stretching exercises, or meditation. Get regular exercise. Avoid excessive alcohol and drug use. Many people think of exercise as just being for weight loss but it does far more than that. It helps combat disease such as high blood pressure, depression, certain types of cancer, stroke, heart disease, and type 2 diabetes.
